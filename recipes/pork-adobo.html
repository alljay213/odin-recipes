Pork Adobo

Description

Pork adobo is a popular Filipino dish made by marinating and simmering pork in a savory mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. The slow cooking process allows the meat to absorb the tangy and umami-rich flavors, resulting in tender, juicy pork with a slightly caramelized exterior. Traditionally served with steamed rice, pork adobo is known for its rich, bold taste and balance of salty, sour, and slightly sweet notes, making it a beloved comfort food in Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds (about 700 grams) pork belly or shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar (white vinegar or cane vinegar)
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed or minced
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, for a touch of sweetness)
  • 1 medium onion, sliced (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Steps

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a large pot or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the pork chunks and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Add the garlic and onion (if using) and saut√© until the garlic is fragrant and the onion is soft.
  4. Pour in the soy sauce and vinegar. Add the bay leaves, black peppercorns, and sugar (if using). Do not stir.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and cover. Let it simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the pork is tender.
  6. Remove the cover, add water, and stir. Continue to simmer for another 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce has reduced and thickened to your liking.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Serve hot over steamed rice.
