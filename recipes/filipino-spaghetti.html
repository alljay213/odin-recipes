Filipino Spaghetti

Description

Filipino spaghetti is a unique twist on the classic Italian dish, characterized by its sweet and savory flavor profile. Unlike traditional spaghetti, Filipino spaghetti sauce includes banana ketchup, which adds a distinctive sweetness, along with ground meat (usually pork or beef), hot dogs, onions, and bell peppers. The sauce is often simmered with tomato sauce, sugar, and cheese to create a rich and creamy texture. Filipino spaghetti is a favorite at celebrations and gatherings, especially for children’s birthday parties, and is typically served with a generous sprinkle of grated cheese on top.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (about 450 grams) spaghetti noodles
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 pound (about 225 grams) ground pork or beef
  • 1 cup hot dogs, sliced
  • 1 cup banana ketchup
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup water or beef broth
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (cheddar or processed cheese)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Sauté the onions and garlic until softened and fragrant.
  3. Add the ground pork or beef and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon.
  4. Add the sliced hot dogs and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until they start to brown.
  5. Pour in the banana ketchup, tomato sauce, and tomato paste. Stir well to combine.
  6. Add the sugar and water (or beef broth) and bring the mixture to a simmer. Adjust the sweetness to your taste.
  7. Let the sauce simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Add the cooked spaghetti noodles to the sauce and toss to coat evenly.
  10. Transfer the spaghetti to a serving dish and sprinkle grated cheese on top.
  11. Serve hot and enjoy your Filipino spaghetti!
