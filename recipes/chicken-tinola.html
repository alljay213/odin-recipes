Chicken Tinola

Description

Chicken tinola is a traditional Filipino soup known for its light and refreshing flavor. This dish features tender chicken pieces simmered in a broth infused with ginger, garlic, and onions, creating a warm, aromatic base. It's typically cooked with green papaya or chayote, which absorbs the savory broth, and fresh leafy greens like malunggay (moringa leaves) or spinach. The result is a comforting, nutrient-rich soup that is often served with steamed rice and a side of fish sauce with chili. Chicken tinola is popular for its soothing qualities, making it a staple comfort food in Filipino households.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds (about 700 grams) chicken pieces (drumsticks, thighs, or cut-up whole chicken)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, julienned
  • 4 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 medium green papaya or 2 chayote, peeled and sliced into wedges
  • 1 bunch fresh spinach or malunggay leaves (moringa leaves)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis), or to taste
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. Saut√© the onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant and the onions are translucent.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they are lightly browned on all sides.
  3. Pour in the water or chicken broth, then bring to a boil. Lower the heat and let it simmer for about 20 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through.
  4. Add the green papaya or chayote slices to the pot. Continue to simmer for another 5-10 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference.
  6. Add the fresh spinach or malunggay leaves and cook for another 2 minutes, just until the leaves are wilted.
  7. Remove from heat and serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy your chicken tinola!
